Open Storage Pod

We are an open source hardware project that is developing affordable petabyte-scale storage servers, built from commodity components. Our current goal is to crack the petabyte-per-rack barrier — which translates into a storage density of 100 terabyte per pod — while consuming as little energy as possible. Our mission can be described as “challenging the server tax” on enterprise storage solutions.

It all started with a bang when Backblaze — an online backup service — open-sourced their custom storage server design in September 2009:

Petabytes on a budget: How to build cheap cloud storage

“We had to figure out how to store hundreds of petabytes of customer data in a reliable, scalable way — and keep our costs low. After looking at several overpriced commercial solutions, we decided to build our own custom Backblaze Storage Pods: 67 terabyte 4U servers for $7,867.” — Tim Nufire, Backblaze

If you want to build your own storage pods and participate in the evolution of the server design, then join our Google group.
